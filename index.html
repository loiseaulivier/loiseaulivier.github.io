About

I am a Postdoctoral Research Fellow at Trinity College Dublin, funded by Taighde Éireann – Research Ireland. My research sits at the intersection of syntax and language change, with a focus on the Romance languages. I have worked on a range of varieties including Old/Modern French, Old Occitan, Old/Modern Italian, Old Sardinian, Old/Modern Catalan, and Romanian, as well as Standard Greek and the Amazigh languages.

I am an Associate Member of the Faculty of Linguistics, Philology and Phonetics at the University of Oxford, and an Associate Researcher of the Maison Française d'Oxford. I sit on the Executive Committee of the Societas Linguistica Europaea.

Before joining TCD, I was a Lecturer in Linguistics at the University of Oxford, where I served as Linguistics Organising Tutor at St John's College, St Catherine's College, and Pembroke College. I previously taught at Queen's University Belfast and Ulster University.

Research

My research investigates the formal properties of natural language, drawing on fieldwork, experimental methods, and cross-linguistic comparison. Current projects include:

Syntax & Phrase Structure

How clauses are built from smaller pieces, and what this reveals about universal grammar.

Morphosyntax

The interaction between word-internal structure and syntactic derivations.

Semantics & Pragmatics

Compositionality, scope, tense, and how meaning is assembled from form.

Language X Fieldwork

Documenting and analysing [Language name], an under-described language of [region].
